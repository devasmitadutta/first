Address to the Nation by the Chief of the Interim Government:

Official Document

Comprehensive National Stability Plan

 Khitat Al-Istiqrar Al-Watani Al-Shamil

Date: June 25, 2011
Location: Al-Qarib, Capital City of the Republic of Al-Qariya

My dear brothers and sisters of Al-Qariya,

 Does Al-Qariya still exist as a state?

Today, our borders are porous, our economy is paralyzed, and our unity is fractured.The question many are asking is simple but terrifying.

You, me, we all know the deep scars left by the events of the past year—the moment when the flames of the Qarab Spring burned through the very heart of our society. We are still reeling from the consequences of those events, but let me recount them once more so that we fully grasp the gravity of our situation, and understand the path we must now walk together.

The Fall of the Old Government: Chaos from Beyond our Borders

It all began to unravel when civil unrest ignited in our neighbouring country, Iman, just across the Qarab Desert. What started as protests against their government’s corruption and repression quickly escalated into full-scale conflict. Armed factions and militias vied for control, and within months, Jabalan, along with Iman descended into chaos. Refugees poured across our eastern borders, seeking safety, while militants from the warring sides began to infiltrate our towns and cities, bringing their violent conflict with them.

The spillover from the Iman conflict took root in our own land. For years, our Qarab brothers have suffered under the corrupt practices of the previous regime, which failed to address economic inequality, rampant unemployment, and the sharp divisions between the northern Qaribs and southern Qarabs. Tensions that had long simmered between our communities exploded into open revolt. The north, wealthier and more industrialised, saw protests over economic disparities, while the south, historically marginalised, demanded full representation and an end to the archaic systemic neglect.

The Coup d'État: A Sudden Shift

The protests in the city of Rahaf began peacefully, but they were met with brutal repression upon reaching our capital  Al - Qarib, by the previous government. The massacre of peaceful demonstrators in the central square at Farj, last August was the final straw. Outrage spread like wildfire, uniting former enemies against the ruling elite. Military defections followed, and within weeks, the government collapsed. The military, with popular support, staged a coup, ousting the Prime Minister and dissolving Parliament.

What followed was not the peace we had hoped for, but a descent into deeper chaos. The very divisions that had sparked the revolution now tore our country apart. North and south clashed, militias took control of towns, and sectarian violence between brothers engulfed entire regions.  Meanwhile, the foreign powers,the Jalabans, having their shrewd interests in our strategic location near the Qarn Sea and the  Imans at  oil-rich lands at the south, began to stoke the fires of division. They funded factions, supplied arms, and sought to undermine our sovereignty. These external forces now lurk in the shadows, threatening to carve up our nation for their own gain.

The Crisis of Statehood: Does Al-Qariya still exist as a state?

The answer, my fellow citizens, lies in our hands.

We are a nation standing at the edge of history, our future uncertain, yet ours to shape.  It is true that we are a nation under siege—from within and from beyond. But as the head of the interim government, I, Faris Al-Qahtani, stand before you today with a plan. A plan to restore law and order, to rebuild our shattered economy, and to reclaim our sovereignty.

We are a proud people. This land, from the golden dunes of the Qarab Desert to the sparkling waters of the Qarn Sea, belongs to us. We will not allow foreign powers, whether they come under the guise of friendship or with open hostility, to dictate our future. Nor will we let the divisions between north and south destroy the unity of Al-Qariya.

Let us,

We the Qarabs and We the Qaribs, 

come together to rebuild, to reconcile, and to reclaim our destiny.

And hence, in the face of such adversity, I present to you the

خطة الاستقرار الوطني الشامل

 Comprehensive National Stability Plan

 Khitat Al-Istiqrar Al-Watani Al-Shamil

I, along with our ministers, have charted this plan not as a short-term solution, but a long-term roadmap for rebuilding Al-Qariya from the ground up. Aims lie  to address the violence, the economic collapse, and the external threats that seek to undermine us. I have outlined a series of initiatives that will guide us toward peace, stability, and the restoration of our al-siyaadah (sovereignty).

عملية الحدود الحديدية

1. Operation Iron Borders (Amaliyat Al-Hudood Al-Hadidiya)

Before anything, let me make this clear: Rahaf, Farj, and Alaim belong to Al-Qariya, and they always will. These cities are not up for debate or negotiation. They are ours by history, by right, and by blood. Anyone—be it the Imans or the Jalabans, militias, or opportunists—who dares to challenge our sovereignty over these lands will face the full strength of our resolve.

As part of the Operation Iron Borders  (Amaliyat Al-Hudood Al-Hadidiya), I am officially declaring our national borders to the world. This is not just a clause in the document; it is a statement of fact. Our borders are non-negotiable. Al-Qariya will not cede a single grain of sand.

The map I present below is our flag on the world stage—let there be no mistake: we will protect our lands, and we will crush any force that seeks to challenge us.

مبادرة الوحدة القارية

2. Qariya Unity Initiative (Mubadara Al-Wahda Al-Qariya)

To begin, we must bridge the divide between the north and south, and between all factions within our society. Under this initiative, we will form a National Unity Council (Majlis Al-Wahda Al-Watani ) composed of representatives from every community. This council will negotiate a ceasefire and guide us toward a new social contract.

At the same time Safe Zones (Manatiq Al-Amn) at Al-Qarib and Darbar will be established in conflict zones, protected by neutral peacekeeping forces.

Further, a nationwide campaign—"One Al-Qariya" (Hamlat Wahdat Al-Qariya)—will promote reconciliation and unity across our land.

And most importantly,in light of the fact that the UN has proved to be ineffective, Malādh, a government developed app, will show where to get food and shelter nearest from your location without internet connection, upon entering your Shaadhhaar Number only.For refugees, announcements via aeroplanes would be made.

خطة إحياء الاقتصاد الدرباوي

3. Al-Qariya Economic Revival Plan (Khitat Ihya’ Al-Iqtisad Darbari)

Our economy lies in ruins. The destruction of the Darbar Port, the collapse of oil exports, and widespread unemployment have crippled us. The Reconstruction of Darbar Port Project (Mashru’ I'adat Bina' Mina' Darbar) will be a priority, restoring our trade routes and revenue streams.

The Qariya Employment Initiative (Mubadara Tajhiz Al-Qariya) will create jobs through infrastructure development and support for small businesses.

And under this, lies a special programme to address our regional disparities. I am launching Fortified Employment Stations (Mahattat Al-Tashgheel Al-Muhassana) in the southern Qarab Desert. The fortified nature of these stations will guarantee safety for workers and businesses, protecting them from the threats of instability while pushing for local growth.

In terms of international trade, we have, even in this chaos, secured and strengthened our age-old relations with the Risaliats and Zaqats. These economic partnerships aim to  provide the aid and investment needed to get our economy back on track.

عملية درع الصحراء

4. Operation Desert Shield (Amaliyat Dir’ Al-Sahra')

External forces continue to threaten our sovereignty, particularly across the southern border with Rahaf, near Imans and Farj near Jalabans, where mercenaries and militants cross into our territory. For this, Operation Desert Shield will fortify our borders with the Qarab Desert, deploying additional forces to secure our territory.

A National Intelligence Expansion Program (Barnamij Tawsi’a Al-Istikhbarat Al-Wataniya) has also been launched to bolster our ability to detect and neutralise foreign threats. This force will work together with the Strategic Communications Task Force (Quwa Takhlisat Al-Ittisal Al-Istratigi) to combat the foreign propaganda that seeks to sow division among our people.

حوار القارية الوطني والمصالحة

5. Qariya National Dialogue and Reconciliation (Hiwar Al-Qariya Al-Watani al-Musalaha)

Healing the wounds of this conflict will take time, but it must begin now. The National Dialogue Conference (Mu'tamar Hiwar Al-Watani) will bring together community leaders, religious figures, and civil society to negotiate a path toward reconciliation. A Truth and Reconciliation Commission (Hay'at Al-Haqiqa wal-Musalaha) will be established to investigate past injustices and propose compensation for victims.

عملية إصلاح الدستور القارية

6. Al-Qariya Constitutional Reform Process (Amaliyat Islah Al-Dustur Al-Qariya)

Finally, after having ensured uniform stability from the sands to the sea, we must reform our political system to ensure that such a crisis never occurs again. The Drafting of a New Constitution (Siyaghat Dustur Jadid) will be a participatory process, involving all segments of society. Within 18 months, we will hold a National Referendum on the Constitution (Istifta' Watani 'ala Al-Dustur), laying the foundation for a fair, representative government where reservations will be made for our Qarabi brothers. Our Electoral Integrity Program (Barnamij Nazaha Al-Intikhabat) will make sure that the elections are free, transparent, and beyond reproach.

Our Al-Siyaadah, Our Al-Qariya 

My fellow citizens, the journey ahead will be long and difficult, but I know we can succeed. Al-Qariya is more than just a land; it is a people, a spirit of resilience and pride that no external force can extinguish. Together, we will restore peace to our nation, rebuild our economy, and ensure that our al-siyaadah (sovereignty) remains intact.

I ask for your trust, your patience, and your strength. The future of Al-Qariya depends on all of us. Let us rise to the challenge and show the world that we are a people united, a nation reborn.

May Allah bless Al-Qariya.

فارس القحطاني
